Background

Fox was born to a Bahamian father and a Canadian mother. At an early age, his family moved to the Bahamas. While in his teens, the athlete enrolled for a church-sponsored exchange student program that took him to Warsaw High School in Indiana. His love for basketall led him to join the school’s basketball team, although he was not allowed to play in his senior year due to ineligibility. Despite this, Rick eventually made it to the Indiana All-Star squad.

While studying at the University of North Carolina, Fox led his team to the NCAA finals. This was one of the highlights of his years on the team.

Rick began playing professionally immediately after he graduated from University.

1. Boston Celtics 1991 – 1997

The 1991 NBA draft saw him become the 24th overall pick in the first round. The highlights of his time with the Celtics include being the first rookie starter on opening night, making it to the first All-Rookie team, reaching playoffs, and achieving double-figure scores.

2. Los Angeles Lakers 1997 – 2004

Rick played alongside famous stars Shaquille O’Neal and the late Kobe Bryant. The highlight of his time with the Lakers was winning three consecutive NBA titles from 2000 – 2002. At the end of the 2003 – 2004 season, he chose to retire when he was traded to the Boston Celtics.

Acting career

Fox has acted in countless films. The most notable ones include He Got Game, Mini’s First Time, The Christmas Gift, Holes, and Tyler Perry’s Meet the Browns. He has had roles in popular TV shows, including Oz, One Tree Hill, Dirt, The Game, Tyler Perry’s House of Payne. Single Ladies, Hit the Floor, Shameless, and Big Bang Theory.
