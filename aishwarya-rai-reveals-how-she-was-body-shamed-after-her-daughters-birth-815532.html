Aishwarya Rai Reveals How She Was Body Shamed After Her Daughters Birth

Aishwarya Rai Bachchan and Abhishek Bachchan welcomed their daughter, Aaradhya Bachchan, into the world in 2011. However, in addition to the well-wishes, there was considerable attention directed toward Aishwarya’s post-pregnancy weight gain. The actress has openly addressed this issue on multiple occasions and also shared the positive aspect.

1. Aishwarya Rai didn’t see any issue with her body after giving birth.

EAST NEWS, AFP/EAST NEWS

During an interview, Aishwarya discussed the issue of body shaming. She stated, “It was natural in my case. That was the natural turn that my body took in terms of whether I gained weight or I had water retention or whatever else that goes with the space. I was comfortable, and that’s why I have been who I have been.”

The actress says she made public appearances when it was convenient for her and her baby. If she had considered it a major issue, she would have chosen to stay out of the spotlight or take corrective measures.

2. People shamed her for the double-chin.

Online reports extensively covered the emergence of a “double chin” in her appearance, leading to a wave of astonishment among internet users, who were accustomed to seeing their beloved beauty queen in a notably altered form.

Despite previously garnering praise for her beauty, she faced harsh criticism for not aligning with societal beauty norms. Aishwarya endured an extended period of disparaging comments and condemnation. Nevertheless, she resiliently triumphed over the relentless criticism directed her way.

3. There was a positive side of body shaming.

Aishwarya also acknowledged that she knew there were ways to lose weight, but emphasized that it was not her priority. She noted, “I wasn’t disturbed by it. If people were, well, I guess I hope they enjoyed the drama because I was busy leading a very real life with my baby. I didn’t realize, but I think the most positive thing that came out of it for me was to have so many women come to me and say ’Thank you.’”

4. She is a public figure but also a mother and a woman.

EAST NEWS, Vianney Le Caer/Invision/East News

Aishwarya didn’t intend to, but inadvertently, embarked on a mission by simply being authentic. She demonstrated that pregnancy and the postpartum period are beautiful yet challenging times, emphasizing the importance of disregarding negativity and gossip.

Above all else, your health and well-being should be your top priority. It’s essential to recognize that you are the sole authority when it comes to determining what makes you comfortable. In a world filled with diverse opinions and external pressures, remember that making choices that promote your own physical and mental health should always take precedence.
